Brazil World Cup 2026

FIFA World Ranking #6 · Group C · 5x World Champions

Brazil — the Selecao — enter the FIFA World Cup 2026 ranked #6 in the world, seeking a record sixth World Cup title. Brazil are placed in Group C alongside Morocco (#7), Scotland (#42), and Haiti (#83). As the most successful nation in World Cup history with five titles, Brazil are perennial favourites and carry the weight of an entire nation's expectations every time they take the pitch at a major tournament.

Brazil's qualification through the CONMEBOL South American qualifiers was not without its difficulties. The ultra-competitive 10-team round-robin format tested Brazil's squad depth and tactical flexibility, but they ultimately secured their place in the 2026 field. The qualification campaign highlighted both the exciting attacking talent at Brazil's disposal and the occasional defensive vulnerabilities that the coaching staff has worked to address ahead of the tournament.

The 2026 World Cup represents a critical moment for Brazilian football. It has been 24 years since their last World Cup triumph in 2002, and the current generation — led by Real Madrid's Vinicius Jr. and a host of young stars — carries the burden of ending what many Brazilian fans consider an unacceptably long drought for the nation that gave the world the "beautiful game."

Tournament Outlook & Predictions

Brazil's Group C features one very dangerous opponent in Morocco (#7), who reached the semi-finals at the 2022 World Cup in Qatar and have continued to improve since. The Brazil vs Morocco opener at MetLife Stadium is arguably the most high-profile group-stage match in Group C. Scotland and Haiti complete the group, with Scotland capable of causing problems with their physical, direct style and Haiti representing one of the tournament's feel-good stories as debutants at the expanded World Cup.

Tactically, Brazil are expected to deploy a 4-2-3-1 or 4-3-3 formation that maximizes the creative output of Vinicius Jr. and Rodrygo, both of whom thrive in wide positions before cutting inside to devastating effect. The midfield pairing of Casemiro and Bruno Guimaraes provides defensive solidity and progressive passing, while Raphinha offers relentless energy and work rate on the opposite flank. The emergence of Endrick as a precocious young forward adds an exciting wildcard to Brazil's attacking options.

Most predictions place Brazil as one of the top five favourites to win the tournament outright. Their group is challenging — particularly the Morocco match — but Brazil's talent pool is deep enough to navigate the group stage comfortably. The knockout rounds will be where Brazil's pedigree is truly tested, and their ability to handle high-pressure moments will determine whether the Selecao can end their 24-year World Cup drought.

Head-to-Head Records — Group C Opponents

Brazil vs Morocco

Brazil and Morocco have met 5 times, with Brazil winning 3, drawing 1, and losing 1. They last met at a World Cup in 1998 in France, where Brazil won 3-0 in the group stage. However, Morocco's 2022 World Cup semi-final run signals a dramatically different team than previous encounters. Morocco's organized defensive structure and dangerous counter-attacking ability make them the toughest test in Group C for Brazil.

Brazil vs Scotland

Brazil and Scotland have a storied history, having met 5 times. Scotland famously opened the 1998 World Cup against Brazil in Paris, losing 2-1 with a Tom Boyd own goal proving decisive. Scotland have never beaten Brazil, but their physical, aggressive style and passionate support could create an uncomfortable atmosphere for the Selecao in their final group match.

Brazil vs Haiti

Brazil and Haiti have very limited competitive history. This will be Haiti's first appearance at a FIFA World Cup in the expanded 48-team format, making it a historic occasion for Caribbean football. Brazil will be heavy favourites, but Haiti's qualification story alone is a remarkable achievement, and they will give everything to compete against one of the greatest footballing nations on the planet.

Brazil at the World Cup — History

Brazil have won the FIFA World Cup a record five times: 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, and 2002. They are the only nation to have qualified for every World Cup since the tournament's inception in 1930 — an unbroken streak of 22 consecutive tournaments heading into 2026. No other country can match this record of consistent excellence across nearly a century of international football.

The 1970 Brazil team, featuring Pele, Jairzinho, Rivelino, and Tostao, is widely considered the greatest football team ever assembled. Their 4-1 victory over Italy in the final in Mexico City remains one of the most iconic matches in sporting history. Brazil's 2002 triumph, led by the "three Rs" — Ronaldo, Rivaldo, and Ronaldinho — saw them win all seven matches en route to the title, with Ronaldo scoring both goals in the 2-0 final win over Germany.

Since 2002, Brazil's World Cup campaigns have been marked by frustration. They were knocked out in the quarter-finals in 2006 and 2010, suffered the infamous "Mineirazo" — a 7-1 semi-final humiliation by Germany — on home soil in 2014, and exited at the quarter-final stage in 2018 and 2022. The 2026 World Cup represents a chance to restore Brazilian dominance and add a historic sixth star to the yellow jersey.

Brazil's World Cup legacy extends beyond trophies. The nation has produced some of the most legendary players in football history, including Pele, Garrincha, Zico, Romario, Ronaldo, Ronaldinho, Kaka, and Neymar. The attacking flair and joyful playing style associated with Brazilian football — jogo bonito (the beautiful game) — has inspired generations of players and fans worldwide.

Brazil World Cup 2026 FAQ

What group is Brazil in at the 2026 World Cup?

Brazil is in Group C with Morocco, Scotland, and Haiti at the FIFA World Cup 2026.

When does Brazil play their first World Cup 2026 match?

Brazil's opening match is on June 13, 2026 against Morocco at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey (6:00 PM ET). This is one of the most anticipated group-stage fixtures of the entire tournament.

How many World Cups has Brazil won?

Brazil has won the FIFA World Cup a record five times: 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, and 2002. They are the most successful nation in World Cup history and the only country to have competed in every edition of the tournament.

Who is the Brazil captain at the 2026 World Cup?

Marquinhos, the PSG and Brazil center-back, is expected to captain the Selecao at the 2026 World Cup. The experienced defender has been a mainstay of the squad and provides leadership and organizational skills at the back.

Is Neymar in Brazil's 2026 World Cup squad?

Neymar's inclusion in Brazil's 2026 World Cup squad depends on his fitness and form. After injuries disrupted his career in recent years, the coaching staff will assess whether the former Barcelona and PSG star can contribute meaningfully. Regardless, Brazil's attack is well-stocked with Vinicius Jr., Rodrygo, Raphinha, and Endrick leading the forward line.

Where does Brazil play their 2026 World Cup matches?

Brazil play at MetLife Stadium (East Rutherford, NJ) on Matchday 1 and SoFi Stadium (Los Angeles, CA) on Matchday 2. The Matchday 3 venue is to be confirmed. Brazil's large diaspora in the United States ensures strong fan support at all venues.
