Morocco World Cup 2026

FIFA World Ranking #7 · Group C · CAF

Morocco enters the 2026 FIFA World Cup as the seventh-ranked team in the world and one of the most compelling stories in modern football. The Atlas Lions rewrote history at the 2022 World Cup in Qatar, becoming the first African and first Arab nation ever to reach the semi-finals of a World Cup. That extraordinary run — which saw Morocco defeat Belgium, Spain, and Portugal on their way to the last four — transformed the nation from perennial underdogs into genuine contenders on the world stage. Head coach Walid Regragui has built a side that combines elite European club experience with fierce national pride and tactical discipline.

Placed in Group C alongside Brazil (#5), Scotland (#42), and Haiti (#83), Morocco face a challenging but navigable path to the knockout rounds. The headline fixture against Brazil on Matchday 1 is one of the most anticipated group-stage clashes of the entire tournament. Morocco's qualification through the CAF qualifying pathway was dominant, as the Atlas Lions topped their group with relative ease and showed the consistency expected of a top-ten side in the world rankings.

The 2026 World Cup represents a chance for Morocco to prove that their 2022 heroics were no fluke. With a squad packed with players competing at the highest levels of European football — from Paris Saint-Germain and Manchester United to Galatasaray and Marseille — the Atlas Lions have the quality and depth to compete with any team in the tournament. Their passionate fanbase, which created one of the most electric atmospheres at the 2022 World Cup, will travel in huge numbers to North America to support the team once again.

Tournament Outlook & Predictions

Morocco's tournament outlook in 2026 is defined by one word: ambition. After reaching the semi-finals in Qatar, anything less than a knockout-round appearance would be considered a disappointment. The Atlas Lions are no longer content to simply participate — they expect to compete for a place in the latter stages of the competition. Their FIFA ranking of #7 is the highest ever achieved by an African nation, and it reflects the genuine quality running through this squad from goalkeeper to striker.

Tactically, Walid Regragui's Morocco are built on a foundation of defensive solidity and rapid transitions. The 2022 World Cup run was characterized by extraordinary defensive discipline — Morocco conceded just one goal in open play across the entire tournament (an own goal against Canada in the group stage). The back line, marshalled by Achraf Hakimi and Nayef Aguerd, is among the most organized in world football. In front of them, Sofyan Amrabat's tireless midfield work provides a platform for the creative talents of Hakim Ziyech and Azzedine Ounahi to operate with freedom on the counter-attack.

The Group C opener against Brazil is the match that will likely define Morocco's tournament. A positive result — whether a draw or a victory — would put the Atlas Lions in an excellent position to qualify from the group with games against Scotland and Haiti to follow. Morocco's experience of big-game environments from 2022 gives them a psychological edge that few other teams outside the traditional elite can match. They have already proven they can beat top-tier European and South American opposition under the most intense pressure imaginable.

One area where Morocco have improved since 2022 is their attacking output. While the defensive record in Qatar was extraordinary, the team sometimes lacked a cutting edge in the final third. The emergence of Bilal El Khannouss as a dynamic creative force, combined with Youssef En-Nesyri's aerial threat and movement, gives Regragui more options in attack. If Morocco can maintain their defensive excellence while adding greater goal threat, they have the tools to go even further than the semi-finals this time around.

Head-to-Head Records — Group C Opponents

Morocco vs Brazil

Morocco and Brazil have met five times in senior international football, with Brazil winning four of those encounters and Morocco claiming one victory. Their most notable World Cup meeting came in the 1998 group stage in France, where Brazil won 3-0 with a dominant display. However, Morocco famously defeated Brazil 2-1 in a friendly in Tangier in March 2023, just months after their historic 2022 World Cup run, demonstrating that the gap between the two sides has narrowed considerably. This Group C opener promises to be one of the most fascinating matchups of the entire group stage, pitting Morocco's disciplined defensive structure against Brazil's extraordinary attacking talent.

Morocco vs Scotland

Morocco and Scotland have had very few senior-level meetings, making this a rare and intriguing fixture. Scotland's physical, direct style of play contrasts with Morocco's technical and tactical approach. Morocco will be strong favourites for this Matchday 2 encounter based on their FIFA ranking advantage and recent tournament pedigree. However, Scotland's battling qualities and the tactical acumen of their coaching staff mean this is unlikely to be a straightforward match for the Atlas Lions. The Moroccan midfield's ability to control possession and tempo will be crucial in dictating this contest.

Morocco vs Haiti

Morocco and Haiti have rarely met at the international level, and this will be their first encounter at a World Cup. On paper, this is the most favourable fixture for Morocco in Group C, with a significant gap in FIFA rankings between the two sides. However, Morocco's coaching staff will be wary of complacency, particularly if the group situation is already settled by Matchday 3. Haiti's CONCACAF qualifying campaign showed they are capable of competing with spirit and intensity, and cup competitions can produce unexpected results when underdogs have nothing to lose.

Morocco at the FIFA World Cup — History

Morocco have appeared at six FIFA World Cups, making them one of the most successful African nations in the history of the tournament. Their first appearance came in 1970 in Mexico, where they became the first African team to earn a point at the World Cup with a 1-1 draw against Bulgaria. Morocco's most celebrated pre-2022 campaign was at the 1986 World Cup in Mexico, where they became the first African nation ever to top a World Cup group — finishing above England, Poland, and Portugal in Group F before losing to West Germany in the Round of 16.

The 2022 World Cup in Qatar transformed Moroccan football forever. Under Walid Regragui, the Atlas Lions embarked on a run that captivated the entire world. They topped Group F with victories over Belgium and Canada and a draw with Croatia. In the Round of 16, they defeated Spain on penalties in a famous tactical masterclass. The quarter-final brought another historic victory, this time against Cristiano Ronaldo's Portugal, with Youssef En-Nesyri's towering header sealing a 1-0 win. Morocco became the first African and first Arab nation to reach a World Cup semi-final, where they eventually fell to defending champions France 2-0. They finished fourth after losing the third-place match to Croatia, but their achievement had already transcended the sport, inspiring an entire continent and region.

Morocco's World Cup history also includes appearances in 1994 (group stage exit) and 1998 (group stage exit despite a memorable 3-0 victory over Scotland). The 2018 World Cup in Russia saw Morocco compete admirably in a tough group with Spain, Portugal, and Iran but exit at the group stage. The trajectory from 2018 to 2022 to 2026 represents one of the most remarkable upward arcs in the history of international football, and Morocco enter the 2026 tournament determined to prove that they belong permanently among the world's elite footballing nations.
