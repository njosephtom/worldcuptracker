Argentina at the FIFA World Cup — History

Argentina has won the FIFA World Cup three times: in 1978 (hosted in Argentina), 1986 (Mexico), and most recently in 2022 (Qatar) — captained by Lionel Messi. The 2022 triumph ended a 36-year wait and cemented Messi's legacy as the greatest player of all time.

As defending champions entering 2026 with the #1 FIFA ranking, Argentina World Cup 2026 expectations are sky-high. They aim to become only the third nation ever to win back-to-back World Cups after Italy (1934, 1938) and Brazil (1958, 1962).
