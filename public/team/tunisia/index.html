The final group match against Japan promises to be a tactically nuanced encounter between two teams that prioritize technical football and intelligent pressing. Tunisia's experience and defensive nous versus Japan's attacking dynamism and squad depth will likely determine which team has the edge. Pundits generally predict Tunisia will finish fourth in Group F, but the Eagles of Carthage have defied predictions before, and their World Cup record shows they are capable of producing individual results that shock the footballing world.

World Cup History

Tunisia hold a special place in African World Cup history as the first African nation to win a match at the tournament. At the 1978 World Cup in Argentina, Tunisia defeated Mexico 3-1 in the group stage, a result that resonated across the continent and demonstrated that African teams could compete and succeed on the world stage. That pioneering 1978 squad, which also drew with West Germany, laid the foundation for Tunisia's identity as one of Africa's most consistent World Cup participants and inspired generations of African footballers to dream of competing at the highest level.

Following that historic 1978 campaign, Tunisia qualified for the World Cup in 1998 (France), 2002 (South Korea/Japan), 2006 (Germany), and 2018 (Russia), making them one of Africa's most frequent World Cup participants. While group-stage exits have been the norm, Tunisia have consistently been competitive, often requiring only a single result to advance but falling just short. The 2006 World Cup saw them produce a strong performance against Spain, and the 2018 campaign included a narrow 2-1 loss to England decided by a Harry Kane goal in injury time. These fine margins have defined Tunisia's World Cup narrative — always competitive, occasionally brilliant, but lacking the sustained consistency to progress beyond the group stage.

The 2022 World Cup in Qatar was arguably Tunisia's most impressive campaign. The Eagles of Carthage defeated France 1-0 in their final group match, becoming the first African nation to beat France at a World Cup. Although they were eliminated on goal difference despite this famous victory, the result validated Tunisia's belief that they belong among the world's competitive footballing nations. The experience and confidence gained from that campaign carry forward into 2026, where a more mature and tournament-hardened squad will seek to convert individual match performances into sustained group-stage success and a long-awaited place in the knockout rounds.

Yes, Tunisia defeated France 1-0 in their final Group D match at the 2022 World Cup in Qatar. Despite this historic victory, Tunisia were eliminated on goal difference after Australia's simultaneous victory over Denmark, falling agonizingly short of advancing to the knockout rounds.
