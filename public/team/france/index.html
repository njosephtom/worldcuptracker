France World Cup 2026

FIFA World Ranking #3 · Group I · 2x World Champions

France — Les Bleus — enter the FIFA World Cup 2026 ranked #3 in the world and as one of the outright tournament favourites. France are placed in Group I alongside Senegal (#15), Norway (#31), and Iraq (#57). The 2018 World Champions and 2022 World Cup runners-up are gunning for a third World Cup title, which would tie them with Argentina and put them behind only Brazil (5) and Germany (4) in the all-time standings.

France qualified for the 2026 World Cup through the UEFA European qualifying campaign, navigating a competitive group with the consistency and quality expected of a team of their calibre. Under head coach Didier Deschamps — who has managed France since 2012 and delivered the 2018 World Cup title — the team has maintained its status as one of the most feared sides in international football. The qualification campaign showcased France's remarkable squad depth, with Deschamps able to rotate freely without any dip in performance.

The narrative heading into 2026 is one of redemption and ambition. France came agonizingly close to retaining the World Cup in Qatar 2022, losing the final to Argentina on penalties after a breathtaking 3-3 draw in which Kylian Mbappe scored a hat-trick. That devastating defeat has fuelled France's determination to go one better in 2026, and the squad believes they have unfinished business on the world's biggest stage.

Tournament Outlook & Predictions

France's Group I is manageable on paper, though Senegal present a genuine test. The Lions of Teranga reached the quarter-finals of the 2022 World Cup and are the reigning African champions, making France vs Senegal one of the most intriguing group-stage fixtures. Norway, powered by Erling Haaland, are dangerous opponents with the Premier League's most prolific striker leading their attack. Iraq, making a rare World Cup appearance, will be competitive but are the clear underdogs in the group.

Tactically, France possess perhaps the most terrifying attacking talent in the tournament. Kylian Mbappe, now at Real Madrid, is the fastest and most decisive forward in world football. Antoine Griezmann operates as a false nine or second striker with extraordinary intelligence and work rate. Ousmane Dembele provides unpredictable dribbling and pace from the flanks, while the midfield duo of Aurelien Tchouameni and Eduardo Camavinga — both Real Madrid players — offers a blend of defensive solidity and progressive ball-carrying that few teams can match.

France are typically ranked among the top two or three favourites for any tournament they enter, and 2026 is no exception. Their mix of World Cup-winning experience (several players from the 2018 squad remain), generational talent (Mbappe, Tchouameni, Camavinga), and tactical versatility under Deschamps makes them one of the most complete teams in the competition. The expanded 48-team format means more matches, which could favour France's extraordinary depth.

Head-to-Head Records — Group I Opponents

France vs Senegal

France and Senegal have a memorable World Cup history. At the 2002 World Cup, Senegal stunned defending champions France 1-0 in the opening match — one of the biggest upsets in tournament history. Papa Bouba Diop scored the only goal, and France were ultimately eliminated in the group stage without scoring a single goal. Senegal will relish the chance to repeat that shock result, and their 2022 World Cup quarter-final run proves they are a serious force. France will be wary of this fixture and determined to avoid a repeat of 2002.

France vs Norway

France and Norway have met numerous times in European qualifying and friendly matches. Norway have historically struggled against France, though they have occasionally produced upsets, including a famous 2-1 friendly win in 2000. The presence of Erling Haaland makes Norway dangerous — the Manchester City striker is one of the most feared forwards on the planet, and a single moment of brilliance from him could change the dynamic of this group. France's defensive quality will be tested against Haaland's movement and finishing.

France vs Iraq

France and Iraq have very limited competitive history. Iraq's qualification for the 2026 World Cup is a remarkable achievement for a nation that has faced enormous challenges in developing its football infrastructure. While France will be heavy favourites, Iraq's passionate fan base and collective determination could make this a more competitive fixture than the ranking gap suggests. This Matchday 2 fixture at AT&T Stadium in Dallas should give France an opportunity to build momentum heading into the group decider.

France at the FIFA World Cup — History

France have won the FIFA World Cup twice: in 1998 as hosts and in 2018 in Russia. The 1998 triumph, led by Zinedine Zidane, was a defining moment for French sport and society. Zidane's two headers in the 3-0 final victory over Brazil at the Stade de France in Saint-Denis became one of the most iconic images in World Cup history, and the multiracial squad — celebrated as "Black, Blanc, Beur" (Black, White, Arab) — became a symbol of French unity and diversity.

The 2018 World Cup in Russia saw a young, dynamic France team — featuring the 19-year-old Kylian Mbappe — storm through the tournament with attacking brilliance and defensive solidity. They defeated Croatia 4-2 in one of the most entertaining finals in recent memory, with goals from Griezmann, Pogba, and Mbappe. At the 2022 World Cup in Qatar, France came within a penalty shootout of retaining the title, losing to Argentina in the final despite Mbappe's extraordinary hat-trick.

France have appeared in 17 World Cup tournaments and have reached the semi-finals or better on seven occasions. They were runners-up in 2006 (losing to Italy on penalties in the final) and 2022. Beyond World Cup success, France have won the European Championship twice (1984, 2000) and the Confederations Cup twice (2001, 2003), establishing themselves as one of the most decorated national teams in football history.

French football has produced an extraordinary lineage of legendary players: Michel Platini, Zinedine Zidane, Thierry Henry, Patrick Vieira, Lilian Thuram, and now Kylian Mbappe. The depth of talent produced by French academies — particularly the famed Clairefontaine training centre — ensures that France remain competitive at the highest level generation after generation.

France World Cup 2026 FAQ

What group is France in at the 2026 World Cup?

France is in Group I with Senegal, Norway, and Iraq at the FIFA World Cup 2026.

When does France play their first World Cup 2026 match?

France open their World Cup 2026 campaign on June 16 against Senegal at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles (3:00 PM ET / 9:00 PM CET). This is a rematch of the famous 2002 World Cup upset where Senegal defeated defending champions France 1-0.

How many World Cups has France won?

France have won the FIFA World Cup twice: in 1998 (as hosts, defeating Brazil 3-0 in the final) and in 2018 (defeating Croatia 4-2 in Russia). They were also runners-up in 2006 and 2022.

Who is the France captain at the 2026 World Cup?

Kylian Mbappe is expected to captain France at the 2026 World Cup. The Real Madrid forward took over the captaincy from Hugo Lloris and is the team's most influential player and leading scorer. At 27, Mbappe is entering his prime and is one of the favorites for the Golden Boot award.

Where does France play their 2026 World Cup matches?

France play at SoFi Stadium (Los Angeles) on Matchday 1 and AT&T Stadium (Dallas) on Matchday 2. The Matchday 3 venue against Norway is to be confirmed. France's large fan base in North America and the significant French diaspora ensure strong support at all venues.

Is France the favourite to win the 2026 World Cup?

France are among the top three favourites alongside Argentina and England. Their combination of World Cup-winning experience, generational talent (Mbappe, Tchouameni, Camavinga), squad depth, and tactical versatility under Didier Deschamps makes them a formidable contender. The pain of the 2022 final defeat has only strengthened their motivation.
