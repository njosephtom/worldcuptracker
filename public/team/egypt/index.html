Egypt at World Cup 2026

FIFA Rank #29 · Group G · CAF

Egypt, known worldwide as The Pharaohs, are one of African football's most storied and decorated national teams. Representing the Confederation of African Football (CAF) at the 2026 FIFA World Cup held across the United States, Mexico, and Canada, Egypt arrive at the tournament with a squad brimming with European experience and continental pedigree. With seven Africa Cup of Nations titles to their name, more than any other nation on the continent, the Pharaohs carry with them a legacy of dominance that stretches back over nine decades. Their qualification for the 2026 tournament marks a significant achievement and signals a new chapter in Egyptian football's long and complicated relationship with the World Cup.

Leading the charge for Egypt is none other than Mohamed Salah, the Liverpool superstar widely regarded as one of the greatest African footballers of all time. Salah's blistering pace, clinical finishing, and relentless work ethic have made him a household name across the globe. At the peak of his powers, Salah brings not only world-class ability but also the kind of inspirational leadership that can galvanize an entire nation. His performances in the Premier League over the past several seasons have been nothing short of extraordinary, and the Egyptian Football Association and supporters alike are banking on Salah to deliver magical moments on the grandest stage of all. For Salah personally, this World Cup represents perhaps his best and possibly last opportunity to lead his country deep into a major tournament and etch his name even further into footballing history.

Drawn into Group G alongside Belgium, Iran, and New Zealand, Egypt face a challenging but navigable path to the knockout rounds. The expanded 48-team format, with the top two in each group advancing automatically, gives the Pharaohs genuine hope of progressing beyond the group stage for the first time in their World Cup history. The blend of experienced defenders, creative midfielders, and dynamic attackers at the disposal of the coaching staff makes this one of the most balanced Egyptian squads in recent memory. Fans from Cairo to Alexandria, from Luxor to the global diaspora, will be united in support as the red, white, and black takes center stage in the summer of 2026.

Tournament Outlook & Predictions

Egypt's prospects in the 2026 World Cup are a fascinating blend of genuine optimism and cautious realism. On one hand, The Pharaohs possess the kind of star power that can single-handedly win matches at the international level. Mohamed Salah's ability to create something from nothing, to turn half-chances into goals and defensive transitions into devastating counter-attacks, gives Egypt a ceiling that few teams outside the traditional powerhouses can match. On the other hand, the depth of the squad beyond the starting eleven and the team's occasional struggles in high-pressure knockout scenarios remain areas of concern for analysts and supporters alike.

Group G presents a varied set of challenges. Belgium, despite a period of transition following the golden generation led by Kevin De Bruyne and Romelu Lukaku, remain a formidable opponent ranked ninth in the world. Iran, sitting at twentieth in the FIFA rankings, are an organized and tactically disciplined side who have consistently punched above their weight at recent World Cups, holding their own against European and South American opponents. New Zealand, ranked eighty-fifth, represent the most winnable fixture on paper, but the All Whites have shown time and again that they are capable of producing stubborn defensive performances and the occasional upset. Egypt will need to approach every match with the highest levels of concentration and tactical discipline.

The key for Egypt will be balancing defensive solidity with attacking intent. In previous international tournaments, the Pharaohs have sometimes been accused of being overly cautious, sitting deep and relying too heavily on individual brilliance from their forwards. Under the current tactical setup, however, there are signs of a more progressive approach. The midfield pairing of Mohamed Elneny and Emam Ashour provides both experience and energy, while the full-backs are encouraged to push forward and provide width. If the coaching staff can find the right balance between pragmatism and ambition, Egypt have the quality to finish in the top two of Group G and advance to the round of 32.

Defensively, the partnership between Ahmed Hegazi and Mohamed Abdelmonem will be crucial. Both are dominant in the air and comfortable on the ball, and their understanding forged through years of playing alongside each other at the international level could be the foundation upon which Egypt's tournament is built. Behind them, Mohamed El Shenawy is a commanding presence in goal, a shot-stopper of the highest caliber whose reflexes and decision-making have saved Egypt on countless occasions. If this defensive unit can keep things tight in the opening fixture against Belgium and build confidence with a result, the momentum could carry the team through the remainder of the group stage.

Head-to-Head Records

Egypt and Belgium have met on a handful of occasions in international football, though competitive encounters between the two nations have been rare. The most notable meetings have come in friendly fixtures, where Belgium's technically gifted squads have generally held the upper hand. However, Egypt's recent trajectory and the presence of Salah make this a far more competitive matchup than historical records might suggest. The Pharaohs will draw inspiration from their ability to compete with and defeat top-ranked European sides in recent years, particularly during AFCON campaigns where they have faced teams with significant European representation.

Against Iran, the head-to-head record is limited but intriguing. Both nations share a history of passionate footballing cultures and have been dominant forces within their respective confederations. Iran's experience at recent World Cups, including impressive showings against Spain and Portugal at the 2018 tournament in Russia, makes them a side that Egypt will need to take very seriously. The tactical battle between these two disciplined sides promises to be one of the most fascinating encounters of the group stage, with both teams likely to prioritize defensive organization and look to exploit set-piece situations and transitional moments.

New Zealand represents a more favorable matchup for Egypt on paper. The All Whites have historically struggled against top African and Asian sides at the World Cup level, though their remarkable unbeaten run at the 2010 World Cup in South Africa, where they drew all three group matches against Slovakia, Italy, and Paraguay, serves as a reminder that they should never be underestimated. Egypt will be expected to dominate possession and create the majority of chances in this fixture, and a convincing victory here could prove decisive in the final group standings. The Pharaohs' superior individual talent and tactical sophistication should give them a clear edge, provided they maintain focus and avoid complacency.

World Cup History

Egypt's World Cup history is one of pioneering achievement, long absences, and emotional returns. The Pharaohs hold the distinction of being the first African team ever to appear at a FIFA World Cup, qualifying for the 1934 tournament in Italy. In that historic campaign, Egypt faced Hungary in the first round and lost 4-2, but the mere act of participating was a landmark moment for African football. It would take decades before other African nations followed Egypt's lead onto the global stage, making that 1934 appearance a seminal event in the history of the beautiful game on the continent.

After that groundbreaking appearance, Egypt endured a painfully long absence from the World Cup. Despite dominating African football at the continental level and winning the Africa Cup of Nations with remarkable regularity, qualification for the global tournament proved elusive for generation after generation of Egyptian players. The Pharaohs came agonizingly close on multiple occasions, losing crucial qualifying matches and playoff ties that left an entire nation heartbroken. It was not until 1990 that Egypt returned to the World Cup, appearing at the tournament in Italy once again. That squad, featuring legends of Egyptian football, managed to draw against the Netherlands and Ireland but ultimately fell short of advancing from the group stage. Another long wait followed, with near-misses in qualifying campaigns for the 1998, 2002, 2006, 2010, and 2014 tournaments adding to the frustration.

The drought finally ended in spectacular fashion when Egypt qualified for the 2018 World Cup in Russia, ending a 28-year absence from the tournament. Led by Mohamed Salah, who scored crucial goals throughout the qualifying campaign including a last-minute penalty against Congo that sent the entire nation into rapturous celebration, the Pharaohs captured the imagination of football fans worldwide. Unfortunately, the tournament itself proved disappointing as Egypt lost all three group matches against Uruguay, Russia, and Saudi Arabia, with Salah hampered by a shoulder injury sustained in the Champions League final just weeks before the tournament. Despite the on-field results, the experience of returning to the World Cup stage reignited the passion and ambition of Egyptian football, and the 2026 qualification campaign was pursued with renewed determination and purpose. With four total World Cup appearances now on the record, Egypt are looking to make the 2026 edition the one where they finally break through to the knockout rounds and give their enormous global fanbase something truly historic to celebrate.

How many times have Egypt been to the World Cup?

Egypt have qualified for the FIFA World Cup on four occasions throughout their history. Their first appearance came in 1934 in Italy, making them the first African nation ever to participate in a World Cup. They returned to the tournament in 1990, also held in Italy, before a 28-year absence that ended with qualification for the 2018 World Cup in Russia. The 2026 tournament in the United States, Mexico, and Canada represents their fourth overall World Cup appearance. Despite their relatively limited World Cup history, Egypt are the most successful team in Africa Cup of Nations history with seven titles, demonstrating their consistent strength at the continental level.

What is Egypt's FIFA ranking?

Egypt are currently ranked 29th in the FIFA Men's World Rankings, making them the third-highest-ranked team in Group G behind Belgium (9th) and Iran (20th). How many Africa Cup of Nations titles has Egypt won?

Egypt are the most successful nation in Africa Cup of Nations history with a record seven titles, a feat unmatched by any other African country. Their AFCON triumphs came in 1957, 1959, 1986, 1998, 2006, 2008, and 2010, with the latter three representing an unprecedented three consecutive tournament victories that cemented Egypt's status as the dominant force in African football during that era. This continental pedigree is a source of immense pride for the Egyptian football community and has produced generations of talented players who have gone on to compete at the highest levels of European club football, contributing to the development pipeline that now feeds the national team with world-class talent like Mohamed Salah, Omar Marmoush, and others.
