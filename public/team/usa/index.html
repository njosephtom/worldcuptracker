USA World Cup 2026

FIFA World Ranking #17 · Group D · Co-Host Nation

The United States Men's National Soccer Team (USMNT) enters the FIFA World Cup 2026 as one of three co-host nations, alongside Canada and Mexico. Playing on home soil with passionate support across 13 American host cities, the USA are in Group D with Australia (#27), Paraguay (#41), and Turkey (#22).

The 2026 World Cup is a landmark moment for USA soccer — the tournament returns to American soil for the first time since 1994, and fans can watch USMNT matches at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles, Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta, and NRG Stadium in Houston.

USA at the FIFA World Cup — History

The United States previously co-hosted the 1994 FIFA World Cup, which set attendance records that stood for decades. The USMNT reached the Round of 16 in 1994 and have qualified for 12 World Cups total. The US hosted the Women's World Cup in 1999 and 2003.

The 2026 World Cup is the USA's first home World Cup in 32 years. With a young, talented squad playing on familiar turf in front of record US soccer crowds, this is seen as the most significant World Cup in American soccer history.
