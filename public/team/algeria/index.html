If Algeria can replicate that fighting spirit, they could spring a surprise that resonates across the footballing world.

Head-to-Head Records

Algeria and Argentina have met on several memorable occasions in World Cup history. Their most famous encounter remains the 1982 World Cup group stage, where Algeria shocked the world by defeating West Germany 2-1 in one of the greatest upsets in tournament history. That victory was later overshadowed by the infamous "Disgrace of Gijon," where West Germany and Austria played out a mutually beneficial 1-0 result that eliminated Algeria despite their superior performances. The two nations also crossed paths in the 2014 World Cup round of 16, where Argentina needed extra time goals to overcome a stubborn Algerian defense. The head-to-head record between these sides is competitive, with Algeria always raising their game against South American opposition.

Against Austria, Algeria have limited direct history at the senior international level. The two sides have not met in a World Cup before, making their Group J encounter a historic first. In friendly matches, the encounters have been closely contested, with neither side establishing dominance. Austria's recent European form under Rangnick makes them a formidable opponent, but Algeria's experience in high-pressure African and World Cup qualification campaigns gives them the mental fortitude to compete. Both teams will view this match as pivotal to their knockout stage ambitions, adding extra intensity to what promises to be a tactically fascinating contest.

Algeria's record against Asian Football Confederation teams is generally strong, though they have not previously faced Jordan in a competitive fixture. The Desert Foxes have historically performed well against teams from outside of Europe and South America, leveraging their technical superiority and physical conditioning. Jordan's surprise run to the 2023 Asian Cup final suggests they should not be underestimated, and Algeria will need to approach this match with full concentration and professionalism. World Cup History

Algeria's World Cup history is rich with drama, heartbreak, and moments of pure brilliance that have cemented the Desert Foxes as one of Africa's most storied footballing nations. Their debut came at the 1982 World Cup in Spain, where they announced themselves on the global stage with that famous 2-1 victory over West Germany. Goals from Rabah Madjer and Lakhdar Belloumi stunned the footballing establishment, but the joy was cruelly stolen when West Germany and Austria conspired in the final group match to produce a result that sent Algeria home. The injustice of that day led directly to FIFA's decision to schedule final group stage matches simultaneously, a lasting legacy of Algeria's 1982 campaign.

Algeria returned to the World Cup in 1986 in Mexico, and again in 2010 in South Africa, where they competed admirably but failed to advance beyond the group stage. However, it was the 2014 World Cup in Brazil that provided their finest hour in the modern era. Under coach Vahid Halilhodzic, Algeria topped a group containing Belgium, Russia, and South Korea, then pushed Argentina to the limit in the round of 16. Rais M'Bolhi was heroic in goal, making save after save as Algeria defended with incredible organization and discipline. It took an extra-time goal from Angel Di Maria to finally break Algerian resistance, and the team returned home as heroes.

Beyond the World Cup, Algeria's continental dominance was confirmed at the 2019 Africa Cup of Nations in Egypt, where they won the tournament in commanding fashion under Djamel Belmadi. That AFCON triumph, powered by the brilliance of Mahrez, Bennacer, and a cohesive team unit, demonstrated that Algeria belong among the elite of African football. The 2026 World Cup represents an opportunity to build on that legacy and prove that Algerian football can compete at the very highest level. With five previous World Cup appearances and a squad that blends experience with youthful ambition, Les Fennecs are determined to write the next chapter in their remarkable footballing story.
