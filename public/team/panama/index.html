Panama World Cup 2026

FIFA World Ranking #34 · Group L · CONCACAF

Panama return to the FIFA World Cup in 2026 after their historic debut in Russia 2018, where the Central American nation captured hearts with their passionate display on the global stage. Known as Los Canaleros — a nickname inspired by the iconic Panama Canal — the team brings a blend of CONCACAF grit, tactical discipline, and an emotional connection with their fervent fanbase that makes them dangerous underdogs in any tournament setting.

Drawn into Group L alongside England (#4), Croatia (#11), and Ghana (#73), Panama face arguably the most challenging group in the tournament. However, having already experienced the magic of a World Cup in 2018 and with a squad that has matured through multiple CONCACAF campaigns, Los Canaleros are better prepared than ever for the pressures of the global stage. Their qualification through the CONCACAF pathway demonstrated resilience and consistency that belies their FIFA ranking.

The 2026 World Cup holds special significance for Panama as it takes place partly on CONCACAF soil, with matches across the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The proximity of host venues means Panamanian fans will travel in enormous numbers, creating a near-home atmosphere that could prove decisive in tight group-stage encounters. With several players competing in MLS and European leagues, this is the most technically gifted Panamanian squad in history.

Tournament Outlook & Predictions

Panama's tactical approach under their coaching setup has evolved significantly since the 2018 World Cup. The team has shifted from a purely defensive, counter-attacking style to a more balanced system that aims to control possession in midfield while remaining compact and difficult to break down. The 4-2-3-1 formation has become their preferred setup, with two holding midfielders providing a shield for the defence and allowing the fullbacks to push forward when the opportunity arises.

Group L presents a formidable challenge. England are among the tournament favourites with a squad stacked with Premier League talent, while Croatia remain one of the most technically accomplished teams in the world despite their ageing midfield. Ghana, meanwhile, are an unpredictable African side who can beat anyone on their day. For Panama, the realistic path forward runs through the Ghana match and a potential upset against one of the European heavyweights.

The team's greatest strength lies in their collective spirit and defensive organization. Panama concede few easy goals and are adept at making matches ugly — frustrating possession-based teams by sitting deep, winning aerial duels, and hitting on the counter through the pace of their wide players. Set pieces are another weapon, with several tall, physical players who are dangerous from corners and free kicks.

If Panama can secure a result against Ghana and keep their matches against England and Croatia competitive, they have an outside chance of finishing as one of the best third-placed teams and advancing to the Round of 32 — a feat that would surpass their 2018 achievement and cement this generation as the greatest in Panamanian football history. Bookmakers give them long odds to advance, but Los Canaleros have defied expectations before.

Head-to-Head Records

Panama vs England

Panama and England have met once at a World Cup — the 2018 group stage in Russia, where England won 6-1 in a dominant display. Despite the heavy defeat, Panama scored their first-ever World Cup goal through Felipe Baloy, a moment that sent the entire nation into celebration. The 2026 rematch carries enormous emotional significance for Los Canaleros, who will be desperate to show how far they have progressed since that humbling experience.

Panama vs Croatia

Panama and Croatia have limited history in competitive fixtures, with encounters confined to friendly matches. Croatia's technical quality and tournament experience — including their 2018 World Cup Final and 2022 third-place finish — make them formidable opponents. However, Panama's physical approach and defensive discipline could neutralise Croatia's possession game if executed correctly. This is expected to be a tight, tactical encounter.

Panama vs Ghana

Panama and Ghana have rarely met on the international stage, making this a fascinating clash of two footballing cultures — CONCACAF physicality against African flair. Both teams are likely competing for the same third-place spot in Group L, making their head-to-head encounter potentially the most important match of the group stage for both sides. Ghana's pace on the wings could trouble Panama's defence, but Los Canaleros' aerial strength and set-piece threat give them weapons to exploit.

Panama at the FIFA World Cup — History

Panama's World Cup journey is one of the most heartwarming stories in modern football. For decades, Los Canaleros were CONCACAF also-rans, falling short in qualification campaign after qualification campaign. The breakthrough finally came on October 10, 2017, when Roman Torres scored the goal that sent Panama to the 2018 FIFA World Cup in Russia — the country's first-ever appearance at football's greatest tournament. The entire nation erupted in celebration, and the government declared a national holiday.

At the 2018 World Cup, Panama were drawn into Group G with Belgium, England, and Tunisia. Although they lost all three matches — 0-3 to Belgium, 1-6 to England, and 1-2 to Tunisia — the experience was unforgettable. Felipe Baloy's goal against England, a low free kick that beat Jordan Pickford, sparked emotional scenes as players, staff, and fans wept with joy. The team's passionate displays, combined with their travelling supporters' carnival atmosphere, won Panama admirers worldwide.

The 2026 World Cup represents Panama's second appearance on the global stage, and the team arrives far better prepared. The 2018 squad was built on veterans who had dreamed of this moment for over a decade; the 2026 squad includes players with international club experience and a tactical sophistication that the 2018 team lacked. With CONCACAF's expanded representation and the tournament taking place on familiar North American soil, Panama aim not just to participate but to compete — and perhaps secure their first-ever World Cup victory.

Panama World Cup 2026 FAQ

What group is Panama in at the 2026 World Cup?

Panama is in Group L alongside England (#4), Croatia (#11), and Ghana (#73) at the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

When does Panama play their first World Cup 2026 match?

Panama's first match at the 2026 World Cup is against Ghana on June 17, 2026, at Lincoln Financial Field in Philadelphia.

Has Panama ever won a World Cup match?

No. Panama lost all three group-stage matches at their only previous World Cup appearance (Russia 2018). They scored two goals — Felipe Baloy vs England and an own goal in the Tunisia match. A first World Cup victory is a major goal for the 2026 campaign.

Who is Panama's best player at the 2026 World Cup?

Jose Fajardo leads the attack as Panama's primary goal threat, while captain Anibal Godoy controls the midfield. Michael Murillo, who plays for Olympiacos in the Greek Super League, is the most high-profile player in European club football.

How many times has Panama qualified for the World Cup?

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is Panama's second World Cup appearance. Their debut came at Russia 2018 after a dramatic qualifying campaign. They missed the 2022 World Cup in Qatar, finishing fifth in CONCACAF qualifying.

Can Panama advance from Group L?

It will be very difficult given the quality of England and Croatia, but the expanded format (best third-placed teams also advance) gives Panama a realistic path. A victory against Ghana and a competitive showing in the other matches could be enough to reach the Round of 32.
